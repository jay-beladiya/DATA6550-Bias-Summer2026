## Meeting Objective

Review the research completed after the first meeting, resolve outstanding concerns regarding dataset selection, and finalize the dataset for the project.

## Discussion Summary

The meeting started with both members sharing the information gathered since the previous discussion.

Jay summarized the research he found on the Adult Census Income Dataset and confirmed that it has been widely used in studies involving algorithmic fairness, demographic representation, gender bias, and racial bias. He also noted that there are numerous academic references available to support the project findings.

Tirth presented an initial exploration of the Adult Census Income Dataset and demonstrated that the dataset contains sufficient demographic information for statistical analysis, visualizations, and fairness evaluation. The dataset structure appeared clean and well documented, making it easier to analyze using standard Python libraries.

The team revisited the GloVe dataset discussed during the previous meeting. Although both members agreed that GloVe provides an interesting example of bias in language models, they concluded that it would require significantly more preprocessing and NLP-specific knowledge than originally anticipated.

After comparing both options once again, both members agreed that the Adult Census Income Dataset would better satisfy the project requirements while allowing the team to focus on analyzing bias instead of spending additional time on complex preprocessing.

## Final Decision

The team unanimously selected the **Adult Census Income Dataset** as the dataset for the project.

## Reasons for Selection

- Publicly available and well documented.
- Frequently used in fairness and bias research.
- Contains multiple demographic variables suitable for analysis.
- Supports investigation of gender, race, and representation bias.
- Easier to clean and analyze within the project timeline.
- Provides enough opportunities for meaningful visualizations and statistical analysis.
